## Executive Summary

### What v8.0 Accomplished

Milestone v8.0 executed the first computational materials design campaign in this project. Seven phases produced quantitative DFT + Eliashberg predictions for:

- **Track A (Hg multilayer):** Hg1234 (4-layer) and Hg1245 (5-layer) cuprates
- **Track B (Nickelate lever-stacking):** La3Ni2O7 under epitaxial strain (-1%, -2%) with RE substitution (Pr, Nd, Sm)
- **Track C (Hybrid superlattice):** Three cuprate-nickelate Ruddlesden-Popper superlattice candidates

All three tracks produced honest, quantitative results with clear mechanism identification.

### What v8.0 Did NOT Accomplish

**No candidate closes the 149 K gap.** The gap between the best-known ambient superconductor (Hg1223, 151 K via pressure quench) and room temperature (300 K) remains 149 K. This is not a failure of the computational campaign but a genuine physical finding: the phonon-mediated pairing channel in oxide materials has a ceiling of approximately 30-40 K.

**Method:** Pressure quench protocol (PQP) as designed in v4.0-v5.0 (Phases 15-21)
**Measurement:** Zero-resistance Tc at ambient pressure after quench
**Value:** Confirms the 151 K benchmark is real and reproducible before investing in alternatives

**Priority:** HIGHEST -- this experiment has the highest information value per dollar

### Secondary: Sm3Ni2O7 on SLAO Film Growth

**Material:** Sm3Ni2O7 epitaxial film on SrLaAlO4 (001) substrate
**Method:** Molecular beam epitaxy (MBE) or pulsed laser deposition (PLD); target thickness 20-50 nm
**Measurement:** Transport (4-point resistance vs temperature), magnetization (SQUID)
**Value:** Tests whether RE substitution + epitaxial strain cooperatively enhance nickelate Tc; validates the dz2 sigma-bonding mechanism
**Expected outcome:** Zero-resistance onset somewhere between 10 K (pessimistic, phonon-only) and 60 K (optimistic, with spin-fluctuation contribution)
**Status:** No existing data for this specific combination

### Tertiary: DMFT+Eliashberg Computation for Hg1223

**Material:** HgBa2Ca2Cu3O8+delta
**Method:** DFT+DMFT (w2dynamics or TRIQS) + vertex-corrected Eliashberg
**Measurement:** Predicted Tc including both phonon and spin-fluctuation channels
**Value:** Resolves whether the isotropic Eliashberg framework can capture the full Tc when spin fluctuations are included
**Expected outcome:** Tc ~ 80-150 K (based on DMFT+Eliashberg results for YBCO in the literature)
**Computational cost:** 100-1000 node-hours on modern HPC
**Status:** Codes available (AbinitioDGA, w2dynamics); no published DMFT+Eliashberg result for Hg1223

## Credibility Assessment

### Are the computed Tc values credible enough to guide experiment?

**For the phonon-only Tc (31 K for Hg1223, 26 K for Sm3Ni2O7/SLAO): YES, as lower bounds.**

The phonon-only Eliashberg calculation is well-validated for conventional superconductors (H3S: 10% error, LaH10: 11% error from v1.0 benchmarks). For cuprates and nickelates, it systematically captures only 20-45% of the experimental Tc, but it does so consistently. The phonon-only Tc is a reliable lower bound.

**For the estimated full Tc (38-88 K for Sm3Ni2O7/SLAO): NO, not credible for quantitative guidance.**

The "estimated full Tc" is obtained by dividing phonon-only Tc by an estimated phonon fraction. This is a rough scaling estimate, not a first-principles prediction. The phonon fraction itself is uncertain by a factor of 2. The estimated full Tc should be treated as an order-of-magnitude indicator, not a prediction.

**For mechanism identification (n=3 optimal, dz2 enhancement): YES.**

The qualitative mechanisms -- AF inner-plane competition killing n>3, dz2 sigma-bonding enhancement under strain -- are well-supported by both the calculations and independent experimental evidence (NMR, ARPES). These are reliable guides for experimental design even though the quantitative Tc predictions are lower bounds.

## Honest Verdict: What Would It Actually Take to Close the Gap?

The 149 K gap to room temperature requires a material with ambient Tc > 300 K. No known or computed material approaches this. The fundamental obstacles are:

1. **Phonon frequencies in oxides** are limited to omega_log ~ 250-350 K. Phonon-mediated Tc cannot exceed ~40 K for this frequency range, regardless of how lambda is optimized.

2. **Spin-fluctuation pairing** provides the dominant contribution (~75-80%) in cuprates, but it is also bounded. The highest cuprate Tc under pressure is ~160 K (Hg1223 at 30 GPa). Retaining this at ambient pressure is the PQP approach, already demonstrated at 151 K.

3. **Room-temperature superconductivity** likely requires either:
   - A material with higher phonon frequencies (hydrides: H3S at 200 K, LaH10 at 260 K -- but these require megabar pressures)
   - A fundamentally different pairing mechanism beyond phonons and spin fluctuations
   - A breakthrough in metastable-state retention that preserves high-pressure superconductivity at ambient

4. **The most productive next steps** are:
   - **Reproduce the 151 K PQP result** (highest ROI: confirms the benchmark is real)
   - **DMFT+Eliashberg for Hg1223** (quantifies the spin-fluctuation contribution)
   - **Test Sm3Ni2O7/SLAO** (validates the dz2 mechanism and nickelate phonon predictions)
   - **Do NOT invest in more phonon-only Eliashberg calculations for oxides** (the ceiling is established)

## Summary

v8.0 established that **phonon-only Eliashberg calculations cannot close the 149 K gap** for any oxide material. The phonon channel contributes ~22% of cuprate Tc and ~45% of nickelate Tc. The remaining pairing comes from spin fluctuations that require beyond-Eliashberg methods to capture. The Hg1223 benchmark (151 K) remains the top candidate. The best new testable prediction is Sm3Ni2O7 on SrLaAlO4 at ~26 K phonon-only. The three most productive next steps are: (1) reproduce the PQP benchmark, (2) run DMFT+Eliashberg for Hg1223, (3) grow and test Sm3Ni2O7/SLAO films.
