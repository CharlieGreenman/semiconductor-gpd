# Requirements: Pressure-Quench and Metastability Design Beyond Hydrides

**Defined:** 2026-03-29
**Core Research Question:** Given the hydride no-go and the `Hg1223` benchmark pivot, which experimentally anchored metastability and non-equilibrium knobs offer the most credible path toward ambient-retained higher-`Tc` superconductivity, and what next candidate classes emerge when synthesis modeling and engineering control are treated as first-class constraints?

## Primary Requirements

### Pressure-Quench Mapping

- [ ] **PQP-01**: Build an exact parameter-window map for `Hg1223`, including `P_Q`, quench or recovery temperature, reported `Tc`, ambient operating status, and stated reproducibility limits
- [ ] **PQP-02**: Compile at least three additional retained-superconductivity analogs or near-analogs with explicit pressure-history and retention details
- [ ] **PQP-03**: Extract recurring retention features such as hysteresis, sluggish transformation, phase coexistence, or symmetry-preserving transitions from the carried analog set

### Engineering Knobs

- [ ] **KNOB-01**: Build a cross-family matrix of at least six systems and four knob classes, including pressure quench, epitaxial strain, oxygen order or anneal history, and microstructure or other non-equilibrium control
- [ ] **KNOB-02**: For every system in the knob matrix, record which observable is moved by the knob: `Tc`, ambient retention, onset versus zero resistance, lifetime, or reproducibility
- [ ] **KNOB-03**: Identify at least two knob classes that appear transferable beyond a single one-paper anecdote

### Metastability Descriptors

- [ ] **DESC-01**: Build a descriptor scorecard with at least eight retention-relevant descriptors, such as hysteresis, structural memory, phase-boundary softness, layered anisotropy, oxygen mobility, processing sensitivity, bulk-evidence quality, and room-temperature gap
- [ ] **DESC-02**: Test the descriptor scorecard against at least six positive or negative controls drawn from cuprates, nickelates, pressure-quenched analogs, and the repo's hydride failures
- [ ] **DESC-03**: Produce a falsifiable rule set that distinguishes retention-friendly routes from routes that are impressive only under pressure

### Candidate Ranking

- [ ] **RANK-01**: Generate a next-route longlist of at least five candidate classes or route classes with explicit evidence class, physics rationale, and failure mode
- [ ] **RANK-02**: Rank the longlist by confidence, room-temperature gap, knob richness, synthesis plausibility, and practical penalties such as toxicity or fragility
- [ ] **RANK-03**: Select one primary next route and one backup route, while explicitly blocking unsupported consumer-hardware language

### Decision and Validation

- [ ] **VALD-01**: Tie every decisive claim to primary sources or prior repo artifacts with exact dates and operating-condition language
- [ ] **VALD-02**: The final route recommendation must include explicit disconfirming observations and route-specific failure modes
- [ ] **VALD-03**: If no route approaches room temperature credibly, the final memo must say so while still naming the strongest scientific discovery program

## Follow-up Requirements

### Extended Work

- **EXTD-01**: Convert the top route into a concrete computational validation campaign
- **EXTD-02**: Draft collaborator-facing experimental suggestions for the highest-ranked knob-controlled route
- **EXTD-03**: Revisit theory watchlist materials, including ambient carbon or framework predictions, only after the descriptor model is fixed

## Out of Scope

| Topic | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| Claiming a room-temperature finished material | Not supported by the present evidence |
| Device or interconnect engineering | Premature before a much stronger ambient route exists |
| Blind high-throughput global search | Outside the scope of this milestone |
| Reopening a hydride-only candidate hunt | The previous milestone already closed that path negatively |
