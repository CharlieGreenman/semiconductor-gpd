# Requirements: Ambient Retention and Practical Viability for Hydride-Derived Superconductors

**Defined:** 2026-03-29
**Core Research Question:** Can any hydride or hydride-derived pathway supported by first-principles evidence retain superconductivity at ambient pressure, or after pressure quench, and if not, do current constraints rule out room-temperature consumer viability within this conventional materials route?

## Primary Requirements

### Literature and Framing

- [ ] **LITR-01**: Compile a primary-source map of ambient-pressure, near-ambient, and pressure-quenched superconducting hydride or hydride-derived systems published through 2026-03-29
- [ ] **LITR-02**: For every anchor system, record `Tc`, synthesis pressure, operating pressure, stability class, and evidence quality; do not collapse these into a single "pressure" field
- [ ] **LITR-03**: Encode an explicit practical-viability scorecard that ranks pathways by operating pressure, quenchability confidence, `Tc`, cost, and toxicity

### Metastability and Ambient Retention

- [ ] **META-01**: Compute the decompression path for CsInH3 from 5 GPa to 0 GPa, including enthalpy and phonon evolution, and identify the first instability under pressure release
- [ ] **META-02**: Estimate at least one kinetic barrier for CsInH3 decomposition or symmetry lowering during decompression
- [ ] **META-03**: Apply the same decompression / barrier analysis to at least one derivative or alloyed analog, not just the original CsInH3 phase

### Candidate Design and Screening

- [ ] **SCREEN-01**: Build a literature-guided shortlist of at least 6 concrete compositions across at least 3 families: alloyed MXH3, ambient hydrides (Mg2XH6-like or related), and hydride-unit / clathrate-derived frameworks
- [ ] **SCREEN-02**: Screen the shortlist for thermodynamic and dynamic stability at both 0 GPa and a practical synthesis band of 0-5 GPa
- [ ] **SCREEN-03**: Produce a ranked shortlist that includes consumer-relevance metadata: operating pressure, synthesis pressure, quenchability score, cost, and toxicity

### High-Fidelity Superconductivity Validation

- [ ] **EPW-01**: Replace synthetic `alpha^2F` with real DFPT+EPW for the CsInH3 baseline and at least one top new candidate
- [ ] **EPW-02**: Apply anharmonic correction (SSCHA or equivalent) to every candidate advanced as ambient or quench-retained
- [ ] **EPW-03**: Run a fully converged Eliashberg validation, and anisotropic treatment where justified, for any candidate advanced as a practical pathway

### Decision and Pivot

- [ ] **VALD-01**: Test all ambient or consumer-viability claims against the 2025 ambient conventional ceiling result and the 2026 stable-ambient hydride survey
- [ ] **VALD-02**: Determine whether any pathway achieves either ambient pressure with `Tc >= 77 K` or a credible pressure-quench route with retained superconductivity above `150 K`
- [ ] **VALD-03**: If neither condition is met, produce a pivot recommendation that explicitly states why room-temperature consumer hardware is not credible in the present conventional route

## Follow-up Requirements

### Extended Analysis

- **EXTD-01**: Direct experimental pressure-quench synthesis protocols for the top pathway
- **EXTD-02**: Large-scale crystal structure prediction for any especially promising chemistry family
- **EXTD-03**: Pivot to unconventional pairing or non-hydride families if v2.0 closes the conventional route

## Out of Scope

| Topic | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| Consumer-device engineering | Not meaningful before an ambient superconducting phase is established |
| Experimental performance claims | This repo remains a computational and literature-grounded project |
| Exhaustive global structure search | Compute cost is out of scope for this milestone |
| Unconventional pairing as the main program | Deferred unless v2.0 explicitly triggers that pivot |

## Accuracy and Validation Criteria

| Requirement | Accuracy Target | Validation Method |
|-------------|----------------|-------------------|
| LITR-01 | All anchor claims tied to primary sources with exact dates and explicit pressure classification | Source matrix cross-check |
| LITR-02 | Zero cases of synthesis pressure mislabeled as operating pressure | Manual audit of pathway table |
| META-01 | Pressure-release path resolved densely enough to identify the first instability or stable window | Enthalpy + phonon checkpoints along decompression |
| META-02 | Barrier estimates stable within `~0.1 eV/f.u.` under numerical settings | Repeat key points or cross-check method |
| SCREEN-02 | `E_hull` accuracy `<= 10 meV/atom`; phonon verdict stable under grid refinement | Convergence tests and comparison across pressures |
| EPW-01 | Real `lambda` and `Tc` converged to `< 5%` and `±5 K`, respectively | k/q-grid and Matsubara convergence tests |
| EPW-02 | Anharmonic stability verdict based on full renormalized phonons, not frequency scaling alone | SSCHA or equivalent validation |
| VALD-02 | Practical-path verdict must reference exact `P_op`, `P_syn`, `Tc`, and quenchability evidence | Final decision memo |
