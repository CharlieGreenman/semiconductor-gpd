# Roadmap: v3.0 Pressure-Quench and Metastability Design Beyond Hydrides

## Overview

Milestone `v2.0` closed the hydride-only practical route honestly: there is no credible consumer path inside the present conventional hydride frame, and `Hg1223` became the strongest experimentally anchored benchmark after that no-go. This milestone converts that pivot into a discovery program. Instead of reopening failed hydride optimism, it asks which metastability and engineering knobs actually retain or create ambient superconductivity, what descriptors govern those successes or failures, and which candidate classes deserve the next serious push.

## Phase Details

### Phase 11: Hg1223 Pressure-Quench Window and Reproducibility Map

**Goal:** The repo upgrades `Hg1223` from a single benchmark point into a parameterized retention map, while comparing it against the strongest pressure-quench analogs.
**Depends on:** Phase 10 benchmark pivot
**Requirements:** `PQP-01`, `PQP-02`, `PQP-03`, `VALD-01`
**Contract Coverage:**
- Advances: `claim-pqp-window`
- Deliverables: `deliv-hg1223-window-map`, `deliv-pqp-analog-map`
- Acceptance tests: `test-pqp-variables-known`
- Anchor coverage: `ref-hg1223-quench`, `ref-bst-pqp`, `ref-fese-pqp`, `ref-phase10-top-candidate`
- Forbidden proxies: `fp-benchmark-equals-product`

**Success Criteria**

1. `Hg1223` is described with explicit pressure-history variables, not just one quoted `Tc`
2. At least three retained-superconductivity analogs are mapped with their quench conditions and retention behavior
3. Recurring retention features such as hysteresis, mixed phases, or sluggish transformations are made explicit
4. The repo emits a reproducibility note that says what is known, unknown, and likely load-bearing for the `Hg1223` route

**Backtracking trigger:** If `Hg1223` turns out to be too sparse or irreproducible to guide anything beyond benchmark status, Phase 12 must widen the comparator set and lower confidence in the cuprate route accordingly.

**Plans:** 3 plans

Plans:
- [x] `11-01-PLAN.md` -- Hg1223 pressure-quench variable and evidence ledger
- [x] `11-02-PLAN.md` -- PQP analog map across BST, FeSe, and related retained-superconductivity cases
- [x] `11-03-PLAN.md` -- Hg1223 reproducibility and scientific-value memo

**Executed outcome (2026-03-29):** `Hg1223` now has an explicit trust split: it remains the highest-confidence retained-ambient benchmark, but its carried public protocol details are still thinner than the `BST` and `FeSe` analogs. The phase identifies `P_Q`, `T_Q`, structural-memory context, and ambient stability testing as the load-bearing variables to carry forward.

### Phase 12: Cross-Family Ambient-Retention Knob Map

**Goal:** The repo compares pressure quench, strain, oxygen order, and related engineering knobs across families instead of treating each system as isolated folklore.
**Depends on:** Phase 11
**Requirements:** `KNOB-01`, `KNOB-02`, `KNOB-03`
**Contract Coverage:**
- Advances: `claim-knob-transfer`
- Deliverables: `deliv-knob-matrix`
- Acceptance tests: `test-knobs-affect-observables`
- Anchor coverage: `ref-programmatic-approach`, `ref-la3ni2o7-ambient`, `ref-lapr327-ambient`, `ref-la2prni2o7-strain`, Phase 11 analog map
- Forbidden proxies: `fp-knob-name-without-effect`

**Success Criteria**

1. At least six systems and four knob classes appear in one comparison matrix
2. Every knob entry specifies what observable changed, not just that a knob exists
3. At least two knob classes are argued to be transferable beyond one material family
4. The repo states which knob family is most promising for discovery and which is most mature experimentally

**Backtracking trigger:** If the knob map collapses into isolated anecdotes with no observable commonality, Phase 13 must treat descriptor discovery as unresolved and reduce confidence in route transfer.

**Plans:** 3 plans

Plans:
- [x] `12-01-PLAN.md` -- Cross-family knob matrix with exact observable changes
- [x] `12-02-PLAN.md` -- Transferability analysis for pressure quench, strain, oxygen order, and microstructure
- [x] `12-03-PLAN.md` -- Comparator scorecard for knob richness versus scientific maturity

**Executed outcome (2026-03-29):** two knob families survive as real route drivers: `pressure quench`, and `strain plus oxygen control`. Pressure quench remains strongest on benchmark proximity through `Hg1223`, while strain plus oxygen control emerges as the richest discovery platform through the nickelate thin-film route.

### Phase 13: Metastability Descriptor Model and Controls

**Goal:** The repo turns the benchmark and knob evidence into a descriptor model that can actually discriminate positive and negative routes.
**Depends on:** Phase 11 and Phase 12
**Requirements:** `DESC-01`, `DESC-02`, `DESC-03`
**Contract Coverage:**
- Advances: `claim-descriptor-model`
- Deliverables: `deliv-descriptor-scorecard`
- Acceptance tests: `test-descriptor-discrimination`
- Anchor coverage: Phase 11 and 12 outputs, `ref-programmatic-approach`, hydride and framework negatives from `v2.0`
- Forbidden proxies: `fp-descriptor-handwaving`

**Success Criteria**

1. At least eight descriptors are defined with short physical justifications
2. The scorecard is tested on at least six positive or negative controls
3. The descriptor model separates retention-friendly routes from pressure-only mirages better than simple `Tc` ranking
4. A falsifiable rule set is written down, including explicit ways it could fail

**Backtracking trigger:** If the descriptor model cannot separate known positives from known negatives, Phase 14 must downgrade it to an exploratory heuristic instead of a decision-grade tool.

**Plans:** 3 plans

Plans:
- [x] `13-01-PLAN.md` -- Descriptor list and physical rationale
- [x] `13-02-PLAN.md` -- Positive and negative control scoring
- [x] `13-03-PLAN.md` -- Discriminator rules and failure-mode note

**Executed outcome (2026-03-29):** the descriptor model separates retained-ambient positives from the carried hydride negatives. Ambient access, controllability, structural memory, and evidence depth now outrank raw low-pressure hype or raw `Tc` under load in the repo's route-selection logic.

### Phase 14: Next-Candidate Ranking and Decision Memo

**Goal:** The repo ends the milestone with a ranked next-route hierarchy rather than stopping at benchmark admiration.
**Depends on:** Phase 11, Phase 12, and Phase 13
**Requirements:** `RANK-01`, `RANK-02`, `RANK-03`, `VALD-02`, `VALD-03`
**Contract Coverage:**
- Advances: `claim-next-route`
- Deliverables: `deliv-route-longlist`, `deliv-route-decision-memo`
- Acceptance tests: `test-top-route-explicit`
- Anchor coverage: all carried milestone outputs plus `ref-programmatic-approach`
- Forbidden proxies: `fp-consumer-language-without-gap`, `fp-benchmark-equals-product`

**Success Criteria**

1. At least five route classes are ranked with confidence, room-temperature gap, and failure modes
2. One primary route and one backup route are selected
3. The top route is framed as a scientific discovery program, not as a solved product
4. The memo states plainly whether the repo is any closer to room-temperature consumer viability than at the end of `v2.0`

**Backtracking trigger:** If no route stands above the others even after descriptor and knob analysis, the final memo must say the repo now has a better map but no singular next route.

**Plans:** 3/3 plans complete

Plans:
- [x] `14-01-PLAN.md` -- Next-route longlist with explicit evidence class and failure modes
- [x] `14-02-PLAN.md` -- Ranked shortlist with descriptor and knob scoring
- [x] `14-03-PLAN.md` -- Final decision memo and route recommendation

**Executed outcome (2026-03-29):** the milestone closes with a real route hierarchy. The primary route is `Hg1223`-class pressure-quenched multilayer cuprates if optimizing for highest current confidence and shortest carried room-temperature gap. The backup route is strain plus oxygen-controlled bilayer nickelate films if optimizing for discovery leverage and ambient controllability. The repo still does not have a room-temperature consumer-hardware solution.
