# v6.0 Literature Grounding: Gap-Closing Route Expansion Beyond v5.0

## Milestone Decision

The newest primary-source landscape supports a route-expansion milestone, not another pure `Hg1223` bookkeeping pass.

- `Hg1223` remains the best carried retained benchmark at `151 K` after pressure quench.
- The broader `Hg` cuprate family still has the highest known absolute `Tc` headroom among experimentally credible non-hydride routes, including `153 K` zero-resist and `166 K` onset in `Hg1223` under pressure.
- Nickelates have advanced enough to matter strategically: ambient film onset near `63 K`, ambient bulk evidence near `40 K`, pressure-enhanced bilayer films above `48 K`, and single-crystal high-pressure reports up to `96 K`.
- Conventional near-ambient routes remain useful controls, but current evidence does not place them closer to room temperature than the unconventional cuprate or nickelate frontier.

## Why This Milestone Comes Next

- `v5.0` finished the `Hg1223` Stage `A` decision package. The next unresolved question is route choice, not route bookkeeping.
- The repo now needs to decide whether the next gap-closing push should stay centered on `Hg`-family cuprates or whether nickelates should be promoted more aggressively.
- A current, primary-source frontier map can answer that more cleanly than another open-ended literature sweep.

## Source-Constrained Consequences

1. `Hg`-family cuprates still define the smallest known gap to room temperature inside the carried non-hydride set.
2. Nickelates now have the fastest frontier-improvement slope and the richest combined pressure, strain, oxygen, and chemical-pressure control surface.
3. Near-ambient conventional routes remain important controls, but their present evidence profile is still weaker or lower-headroom than the unconventional frontier.
4. The next route-program memo should therefore rank one primary route and one secondary route, not reopen a diffuse all-routes watchlist.

## Practical Guardrail

No source in the carried set supports a room-temperature superconductor. This milestone should treat route expansion as route expansion only, not as a breakthrough claim.
