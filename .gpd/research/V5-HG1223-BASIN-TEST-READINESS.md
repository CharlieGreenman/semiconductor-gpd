# v5.0 Literature Grounding: Hg1223 Basin-Test Readiness

## Milestone Decision

The newest primary-source landscape does not justify changing the repo's route hierarchy. `Hg1223` remains the strongest carried retained-ambient benchmark at `151 K` after pressure quench, while bilayer nickelate films remain the most interesting ambient-control backup but still much lower in `Tc`.

That means the best next milestone is not another broad search. It is the first instrumented `Hg1223` benchmark-window reproduction package with mandatory `vQ` visibility, stage-local thermal accounting, and explicit keep / hold / pivot rules.

## Why `Hg1223` Stays Primary

- The carried `Hg1223` benchmark still defines the smallest room-temperature gap in the repo's retained-ambient route set: `151 K`, still `149 K` below `300 K`.
- `v4.0` already made the route protocol-specified and campaign-defined.
- The largest remaining uncertainty is not candidate ranking. It is whether the benchmark survives instrumented reproduction once `vQ`, sample state, and warm handling are surfaced.

## Why Nickelates Stay Backup

- Ambient-pressure bilayer nickelate thin films remain the best controlled backup platform.
- The carried literature now includes ambient-pressure onset near `63 K` in compressively strained `(La,Pr)3Ni2O7` films, which strengthens the backup route scientifically.
- Even so, that is still far below the retained `Hg1223` benchmark, and the route remains onset-heavy and thin-film-specific.

## Milestone Consequence

The next repo step should answer a narrower but more decisive question:

1. Can the carried `Hg1223` Stage `A` nodes be reproduced under complete `PQ/TQ/vQ` logging?
2. If not, can the misses be localized to target-state preparation, quench trajectory, sample state, or warm handling?
3. If the misses are clean and well-localized, when should the repo strengthen the nickelate backup route instead of extending `Hg1223` optimism?

## Practical Guardrail

No source in the carried set supports a room-temperature superconductor. The milestone should treat better experiment readiness as exactly that: better experiment readiness, not a room-temperature breakthrough.
