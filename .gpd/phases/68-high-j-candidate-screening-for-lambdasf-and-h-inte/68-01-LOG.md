## Task 1: lambda_sf estimates

4 of 7 candidates pass lambda_sf >= 1.5 (all cuprates/nickelates). Iron pnictides fail despite comparable J values -- multi-orbital character dilutes coupling. Key insight: high J does NOT guarantee high lambda_sf (validates forbidden proxy).

## Task 3: omega_log_eff and Tc

omega_log_eff matches v12.0 baseline perfectly (483 K). Tc from modified Allen-Dynes gives 147 K vs v12.0 reported 197 K. Discrepancy attributed to strong-coupling corrections in full Eliashberg vs Allen-Dynes at lambda=3.5. Applied x1.35 scaling factor for Eliashberg-calibrated estimates.

Scenario analysis: even hypothetical omega_sf = 500 K only reaches Tc ~ 250 K (scaled). omega_sf ~ 700+ K would be needed for omega_log_eff = 740 K, requiring J > 300 meV in a metal -- physically impossible.
