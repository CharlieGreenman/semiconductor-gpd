# Phase 18 Final Memo

## Final v4 Verdict

`Hg1223` remains the primary route in the repo.

`v4.0` did not prove a reproducibility basin, and it did not move the route closer to room-temperature practicality. What it did do is convert `Hg1223` from a benchmark slogan into a protocol-specified, control-mapped, campaign-defined research program.

## Route Hierarchy

- **Primary route:** `Hg1223` pressure-quench program
- **Backup route:** bilayer nickelate films with strain plus oxygen control

## Practical Guardrail

- best carried retained `Hg1223` benchmark: `151 K`
- room temperature reference: `300 K`
- remaining gap: `149 K`
- **consumer hardware remains unsupported**

## What v4 Added

- exact retained `Hg1223` window and fragility budget
- shared-versus-route-specific `PQP` control map
- staged reproducibility campaign
- explicit success, downgrade, and stop gates

## What v4 Did Not Add

- new replication data
- a proven basin width
- any evidence for room-temperature operation
- any basis to drop the nickelate backup route

## Next Focus

The next focused effort should be the first stage of the `Hg1223` campaign:

1. low-`TQ` benchmark-window reproduction under recorded `vQ`
2. immediate ambient cryogenic retained-state measurement
3. staged thermal holds before retrieval
4. ex-DAC bulk and structural follow-up on survivors

If that first stage fails cleanly, the route should be downgraded and the nickelate backup should move closer to center.
