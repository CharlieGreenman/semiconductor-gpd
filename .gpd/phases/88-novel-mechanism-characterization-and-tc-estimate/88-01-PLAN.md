# Phase 88: Novel Mechanism Characterization and Tc Estimate

## Objective

Characterize the mechanisms behind Phase 87 anomalies (especially FeSe/STO).
Propose mechanism hypotheses with Tc scaling arguments. Test whether any novel
mechanism can reach 300 K when combined with known physics.

## Context

- Phase 87: 5 anomalies; FeSe/STO is the key case (8x, reproducible)
- Key question: can FeSe/STO principle + Hg1223 base reach 300 K?
- La3Ni2O7 at 80 K under pressure: mechanism under active debate

## Tasks

### Task 1: FeSe/STO mechanism decomposition (type="auto")

Decompose the 8x enhancement into:
- Un-suppression of nematic order (how much of the 8x?)
- Cross-interface STO phonon coupling (how much?)
- Forward scattering enhancement (non-adiabatic, connects to Track A)

Estimate each contribution quantitatively.

### Task 2: Mechanism hypotheses for all anomalies (type="auto")

For each of the 5 anomalous materials, propose the most likely mechanism
and estimate delta_Tc from that mechanism. Build mechanism hypothesis table.

### Task 3: 300 K synthesis -- all tracks combined (type="auto")

Combine findings from Tracks A-D: what is the maximum achievable Tc
when ALL beyond-Eliashberg mechanisms are combined optimistically?
Produce the definitive mechanism table for Phase 89.

## Success Criteria

- FeSe/STO decomposed into contributing factors
- Mechanism hypothesis for each anomaly
- Combined all-tracks Tc estimate with uncertainty
- 300 K verdict with explicit gap analysis
