# Phase 12 Comparator Scorecard

## Knob-Family Scorecard

| Knob family | Discovery richness | Benchmark proximity | Experimental maturity | Main strength | Main weakness |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| pressure quench | high | highest | medium | shortest current room-temperature gap via `Hg1223`; already yields retained ambient states in multiple families | protocol opacity and reproducibility remain serious |
| strain plus oxygen control | high | medium | medium-high | richest tunable ambient platform, especially in nickelate films | current `Tc` is still far below `Hg1223` |
| microstructure and storage control | medium | low | medium | clearly matters for stability and transport quality | secondary knob, not yet a primary route driver |
| chemistry-only substitution | low-medium | low | low | can assist a coupled route | too entangled with other knobs in the carried set |

## Primary Verdicts

- `richest discovery knob family`: `strain plus oxygen control`
- `strongest current benchmark knob family`: `pressure quench`

## Why These Verdicts Differ

The highest-confidence route today and the most discoverable platform tomorrow are not the same thing.

- Pressure quench wins on absolute retained `Tc` because `Hg1223` is the strongest carried benchmark.
- Strain plus oxygen control wins on discovery richness because it gives repeated, experimentally accessible, ambient-pressure tuning in a family where structure, stoichiometry, and interface control are all visible.

That split should remain explicit in later ranking work.
