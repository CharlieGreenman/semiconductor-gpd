<objective>
Screen the B-C clathrate hydrides (SrNH4B6C6, PbNH4B6C6) and Mg2XH6 family (Mg2IrH6 as validation target) for thermodynamic and dynamic stability.

Purpose: The clathrate family offers ambient-pressure dynamic stability with moderate Tc (85-115 K). Mg2IrH6 serves as a VALIDATION target for the hull methodology -- it is dynamically stable at 0 GPa but thermodynamically unstable (E_hull = 172 meV/atom per Lucrezi et al.). If our hull correctly identifies Mg2IrH6 as unstable, we can trust the hull results for the primary perovskite candidates.

Output:
- Formation enthalpies and E_hull for clathrate and Mg2XH6 candidates
- Phonon stability verdicts
- Hull methodology validation via Mg2IrH6
- Stability-ranked candidate list (secondary family)
</objective>

  <name>Task 1: Construct convex hulls for clathrate and Mg2XH6 systems and validate hull methodology with Mg2IrH6</name>
  <files>screening/clathrate_screening.py, screening/mg2xh6_screening.py, data/candidates/clathrate_results.json, data/candidates/mg2xh6_results.json, figures/hull_clathrate.pdf, figures/hull_mg2irh6.pdf</files>
  <action>
    **Part A: Mg2IrH6 hull validation (PRIORITY -- do this first)**

    1. Generate Mg2IrH6 structure (Fm-3m, a ~ 6.8 A from Lucrezi et al.).
       - Mg at 8c Wyckoff (0.25, 0.25, 0.25), Ir at 4a (0,0,0), H at 24e (x,0,0) with x ~ 0.24.
    2. Relax at 0 GPa with PBEsol. Record total energy and volume.
    3. Compute competing phases for Mg-Ir-H system at 0 GPa:
       - Elemental: Mg (HCP), Ir (FCC), H2 (molecular)
       - Binary hydrides: MgH2 (rutile, MUST validate against -75.2 kJ/mol), IrH (if stable), IrH2, IrH3
       - Binary intermetallics: MgIr, Mg2Ir, MgIr2, MgIr3
       - CRITICAL: Include all Mg-Ir intermetallics. Missing MgIr3 could shift the hull significantly.
    4. Build convex hull. Compute E_hull for Mg2IrH6.
    5. **VALIDATION CHECK:** E_hull must be > 100 meV/atom (literature: 172 meV/atom).
       - If E_hull < 50 meV/atom: STOP. Hull is incomplete. Add more competing phases.
       - If 50 < E_hull < 100: Qualitatively correct but hull may be missing phases. Investigate.
       - If E_hull > 100: Hull methodology validated. Proceed.
    6. Also validate: MgH2 formation enthalpy should be within 15% of -75.2 kJ/mol.

    **Part B: Clathrate systems (SrNH4B6C6, PbNH4B6C6)**

    1. Generate structures from Wang et al. prototypes. The sodalite B6C6 cage with NH4 tetrahedra and M atom filling. These are large unit cells (~20+ atoms).
       - For QE calculations of large cells: reduce k-grid proportionally (8^3 or even 6^3 for cells with a > 8 A).
       - ecutwfc = 80 Ry (B, C, N, H are all light elements).

    2. **Pseudo-ternary hull construction:**
       - Treat as (M)(NH4)(B6C6) pseudo-ternary.
       - Competing phases: M metal, NH3 + 0.5 H2 (decomposition of NH4), B6C6 or BC (binary).
       - Also include: M-B, M-C, M-N binaries; BN (cubic and hexagonal); B4C; B-C binaries.
       - IMPORTANT: The pseudo-ternary approximation is a simplification. Document that the true hull is in a higher-dimensional composition space.

    3. Relax at P = 0 GPa (primary -- clathrates are ambient-pressure candidates) and P = 10 GPa (secondary).

    4. Compute E_hull. Report whether candidates are near hull (< 50 meV/atom) or far above.

    5. **Generate hull figures:** Phase diagrams for both Mg-Ir-H (ternary) and pseudo-ternary clathrate systems.
  </action>
  <verify>
    1. CRITICAL VALIDATION: Mg2IrH6 E_hull at 0 GPa > 100 meV/atom (literature: 172 meV/atom). If this fails, hull is incomplete.
    2. MgH2 formation enthalpy within 15% of -75.2 kJ/mol (-64 to -86.5 kJ/mol range)
    3. Pseudo-ternary approximation documented with justification
    4. All competing binary phases for Mg-Ir system included (at least MgH2, IrH/IrH2, MgIr, Mg2Ir)
    5. Clathrate structures have correct atom count matching Wang et al. prototype
    6. k-grid scaled appropriately for large clathrate cells (6^3-8^3 for a > 8 A)
  </verify>

    **Part A: Mg2IrH6 phonon check (methodology validation)**

    Mg2IrH6 is expected to be DYNAMICALLY stable at 0 GPa (Lucrezi et al. confirm this) even though it is THERMODYNAMICALLY unstable. This validates a key distinction: dynamic stability (real phonon frequencies) does NOT imply thermodynamic stability (E_hull < 50 meV/atom). Demonstrating this distinction in our pipeline is important.

    1. Compute DFPT phonons for Mg2IrH6 at 0 GPa on 4x4x4 q-grid.
       - Use relaxed structure from Task 1.
       - ASR='crystal' in q2r.x.
       - Interpolate along Gamma-X-W-L-Gamma-K high-symmetry path for FCC.
    2. Verify: all phonon frequencies should be real (> -5 cm^-1).
    3. Record min_frequency_cm-1 and stability verdict.

    This is NOT a candidate promotion -- Mg2IrH6 FAILS fp-above-hull and does not advance to Phase 3. This phonon check validates that our DFPT phonon pipeline works for Fm-3m hydrides.

    **Part B: Clathrate candidates (if near hull)**

    For each clathrate candidate with E_hull < 50 meV/atom at 0 or 10 GPa (from Task 1):

    1. Compute DFPT phonons on q-grid appropriate for cell size:
       - For large cells (> 10 atoms): 2x2x2 q-grid minimum, 4x4x4 if feasible.
       - CAUTION: DFPT for 20+ atom cells is very expensive. If computational cost is prohibitive, report this and suggest finite-displacement phonons (Phonopy) as an alternative.
    2. Apply ASR='crystal'. Plot phonon dispersion.
    3. q-grid convergence: compare 2x2x2 vs 4x4x4 (or 4x4x4 vs 6x6x6 for smaller cells).
    4. Stability verdict: same criteria as Plan 02-02.

    **If clathrate candidates are ALL > 50 meV/atom above hull at 0 GPa:**
    - Skip phonon calculation.
    - Record: "thermodynamically unstable at 0 GPa; phonon check skipped per fp-above-hull"
    - This is a valid negative result. Wang et al. reported only DYNAMIC stability; thermodynamic stability was not verified.

    **Record all results** to data/candidates/clathrate_phonons.json and mg2xh6_phonons.json.
  </action>

    2. Mg2IrH6: thermodynamically unstable (E_hull > 100 meV/atom from Task 1) -- fp-above-hull correctly blocks advancement
    3. For clathrate candidates: q-grid convergence verified OR computational infeasibility documented
    4. Stability verdicts recorded for all candidates
    5. Correct branch count: Mg2IrH6 has 9 atoms/cell -> 27 phonon branches (3 acoustic + 24 optical)
    6. fp-unstable-tc enforced: no Tc discussion for dynamically unstable structures
