<objective>
Validate the DFT+DFPT+Eliashberg computational pipeline by reproducing the superconducting critical temperature of LaH10 (Fm-3m) at 170 GPa.

Purpose: LaH10 is the second benchmark (ref-lah10) and tests the pipeline on a larger, more complex clathrate hydride (11-atom primitive cell vs 4-atom H3S). Success on BOTH H3S and LaH10 is required to establish pipeline trust (claim-benchmark). LaH10 also has higher lambda (potentially > 3), testing the strong-coupling regime of the Eliashberg solver.

Output: QE input files for LaH10 at 170 GPa; benchmark data (Tc, lambda, omega_log, alpha2F, phonon dispersion); comparison figures; convergence documentation.
</objective>

  <action>
    Set up the LaH10 Fm-3m crystal structure and generate all QE input files for the DFT+DFPT pipeline at 170 GPa.

    1. **Structure setup:** Build Fm-3m LaH10 (space group #225). The PRIMITIVE FCC cell contains 11 atoms (1 La + 10 H). The conventional cubic cell has 44 atoms -- do NOT use the conventional cell for production calculations.

    Fm-3m LaH10 structure (conventional lattice parameter a ~ 5.1 A at 150-170 GPa):
    - La at Wyckoff 4a: (0, 0, 0)
    - H1 at Wyckoff 8c: (1/4, 1/4, 1/4) -- 8 hydrogen atoms forming a cube
    - H2 at Wyckoff 32f: (x, x, x) with x ~ 0.12 -- additional H in sodalite-like cage

    Use ASE or manual construction to build the primitive FCC cell. The primitive cell vectors for FCC are: a1 = a/2*(0,1,1), a2 = a/2*(1,0,1), a3 = a/2*(1,1,0).

    2. **Generate vc-relax input** (simulations/lah10/lah10_relax.in):
       - calculation = 'vc-relax', press = 1700.0 (kbar = 170 GPa)
       - ecutwfc = 80 Ry (test convergence; may need 100 Ry), ecutrho = 320 Ry
       - smearing = 'mp', degauss = 0.02 Ry
       - k-grid: 16x16x16 MP Gamma-centered (smaller than H3S due to larger cell)
       - conv_thr = 1.0d-10, forc_conv_thr = 1.0d-4
       - ONCV PseudoDojo PBEsol pseudopotentials for La and H
       - La pseudopotential MUST include semicore 5s5p states in valence for high-pressure accuracy

    3. **Convergence tests** (simulations/lah10/convergence_test.py):
       - ecutwfc: 60, 80, 100 Ry. Target: < 1 meV/atom between successive cutoffs.
       - k-grid: 12^3, 16^3, 20^3. Target: DOS(E_F) stable to 5%.
       - Document selected parameters with justification.

    4. **SCF and NSCF inputs:** Same pattern as H3S, with converged parameters from step 3.

    5. **DFPT phonons** (simulations/lah10/lah10_ph.in):
       - Start with 4x4x4 coarse q-grid (LaH10 DFPT is ~15x more expensive than H3S)
       - tr2_ph = 1.0d-14
       - fildvscf = 'dvscf'
       - This is the computational bottleneck: expect 24-72 hours on 16-32 cores

    6. **Phonon dispersion check:** Along Gamma-X-W-L-Gamma-K high-symmetry path for FCC.
       - KNOWN ISSUE: Harmonic DFPT for Fm-3m LaH10 may show soft modes (slightly imaginary) at some q-points near 170 GPa. This is because quantum nuclear effects (captured by SSCHA, not harmonic DFPT) stabilize the structure. Per Errea et al. 2020, the harmonic approximation slightly underestimates stability for LaH10.
       - If soft modes appear: (a) Test q-grid convergence (try 6^3 q-grid), (b) Try 175-180 GPa where Im-3m is more firmly stable, (c) Document the soft modes with their magnitude and note that SSCHA stabilization is expected.
       - For the benchmark to PASS, the vast majority of modes must be real. One or two marginally imaginary modes (< 20 cm^-1 imaginary) at specific q-points is acceptable for LaH10 given the known SSCHA stabilization physics.
  </action>
