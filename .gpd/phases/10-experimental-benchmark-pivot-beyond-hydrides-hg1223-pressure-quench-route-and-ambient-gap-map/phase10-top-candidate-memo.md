# Phase 10 Top Candidate Memo

## Top Candidate

`HgBa2Ca2Cu3O8+delta` via pressure-quench protocol

## Why This Is The Winner

The repo now has two different kinds of "best" material:

- highest hydride `Tc` benchmark: `CsInH3` at `214 K` and `3 GPa`
- highest confidence broader benchmark after the hydride no-go: `Hg1223` at `151 K` and ambient pressure after quench

For the user's stated direction, the second category matters more now. A candidate we are actually confident in is more useful than a higher-`Tc` route that still depends on theory-only or pressure-supported assumptions.

## Confidence Class

`high-confidence benchmark`

Why:

- direct experimental report
- ambient-pressure superconductivity after preparation
- highest `Tc` in the carried experimental ambient or retained-ambient set

## Why It Is Not Yet Room-Temperature Consumer Hardware

- `Tc = 151 K` is still `149 K` short of room temperature
- pressure-quench preparation is not the same as commodity manufacturing
- complex oxide processing and reproducibility still matter

So the correct statement is:

- we have found a candidate we can be confident in as a benchmark
- we have **not** found a room-temperature consumer-hardware solution

## Next Route

The next route to investigate should be:

- pressure-quench and ambient-retention benchmarks centered on `Hg1223`
- with `SmNiO2` as the main ambient-oxide comparator
- while preserving `MgB2` as the practical ambient floor and `CsInH3` as the hydride benchmark ceiling

That route keeps the repo grounded in real evidence instead of reopening speculative hydride claims.
