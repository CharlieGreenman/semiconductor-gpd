# Phase 78: Frustrated Magnet Candidate Survey and SF Suppression Assessment

## Objective

Survey correlated materials with geometric frustration (triangular, kagome, pyrochlore lattices) for the combination of suppressed spin fluctuations (low lambda_sf) and preserved d-wave pairing. Establish whether frustration can resolve the correlation/SF tension that caps Tc.

## Context

- v13.0: 300 K requires lambda_ph >= 3.0 + d-wave (mu*=0) + omega_log_eff >= 740 K
- v11.0: cuprate lambda_sf = 2.70, omega_sf ~ 350 K -- these drag omega_log_eff to 483 K
- Key hypothesis: geometric frustration suppresses long-range AF order and the spin resonance energy; if lambda_sf drops below ~1.5 while d-wave pairing survives, omega_log_eff could rise toward 740 K
- Key risk: frustration kills the pairing channel along with the magnetic order

## Anchors

- Cuprate lambda_sf = 2.70 (baseline to beat -- want < 1.5)
- v11.0 CTQMC method validated (146 K vs 151 K expt)
- v12.0 omega_log_eff = 483 K baseline

## Tasks

### Task 1: Frustrated-lattice material family survey (type="auto")
Survey triangular, kagome, pyrochlore, and related frustrated-geometry correlated materials:
- Na_xCoO2.yH2O (triangular cobaltate, existing SC at ~5 K)
- AV3Sb5 kagome metals (A=K,Rb,Cs; CDW + SC at ~2-3 K)
- Fe3Sn2, Mn3X kagome metals
- Cd2Re2O7, Cd2Os2O7 pyrochlore
- kappa-BEDT organic triangular Mott insulators (Tc ~ 10 K under pressure)
- NaNiO2 triangular nickelate
- Additional: herbertsmithite ZnCu3(OH)6Cl2, TbInO3, other spin-liquid candidates
Tabulate: compound, lattice geometry, U/W estimate, T_N/J frustration ratio, existing Tc (if any), electronic structure notes.

### Task 2: Frustration-suppressed lambda_sf computation (type="auto", verify="numerical")
For each candidate family, compute or estimate lambda_sf using RPA chi(q,omega) on the frustrated lattice.
Use a single-band Hubbard model on triangular/kagome/pyrochlore geometries with appropriate U/t.
Compare with square-lattice (cuprate-like) lambda_sf = 2.70.
Key question: does frustration reduce lambda_sf below 1.5?

### Task 3: d-wave pairing eigenvalue on frustrated lattices (type="auto", verify="numerical")
For each lattice geometry, solve the linearized gap equation using the RPA spin susceptibility from Task 2.
Determine the leading pairing symmetry and eigenvalue.
On triangular lattice: d-wave is NOT the leading instability (it's d+id or f-wave).
On kagome: pairing symmetry depends on filling and band structure.
Key question: is there ANY unconventional pairing channel with mu*~0 that survives frustration?

### Task 4: Candidate assessment table and Track C viability (type="auto")
Consolidate results into the required candidate table:
compound | lattice | U/W | T_N/J | lambda_sf | pairing symmetry | d-wave viable? | mu* estimate
Assess whether ANY candidate achieves lambda_sf < 1.5 with a positive unconventional pairing eigenvalue.
If no candidate meets both criteria: document which came closest and why the frustrated-magnet route fails.

## Success Criteria
1. Survey covers >= 6 material families across triangular, kagome, and pyrochlore geometries
2. lambda_sf computed for top candidates; compared with cuprate baseline 2.70
3. Pairing symmetry determined on frustrated lattices; d-wave viability assessed
4. Candidate table produced with all required columns
5. Track C viability verdict with quantitative justification
