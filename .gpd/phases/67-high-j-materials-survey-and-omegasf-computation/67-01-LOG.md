## Task 1: Literature survey

Compiled J values for 35 materials across 13 families. Key finding: only Cu-O systems achieve J > 100 meV. The highest J values in any material are found in 1D chain cuprates (Sr2CuO3: 241 meV, SrCuO2: 260 meV, Ca2CuO3: 254 meV), but these are 1D insulators.

## Task 2: omega_sf computation

**Critical correction during execution:** The PLAN specified omega_sf = 2*sqrt(2)*J*S, but this is the BARE zone-boundary magnon energy, not the dressed spin-fluctuation frequency relevant for superconducting pairing. The v12.0 baseline omega_sf = 350 K = 30 meV is the dressed scale. For cuprates, the renormalization factor f = omega_sf(dressed)/J ~ 0.23.

Applied Deviation Rule 4 (missing component): added dressed omega_sf estimation using material-class-dependent renormalization factors. This is essential for meaningful comparison with the v12.0 baseline.

## Task 3: Feasibility assessment

No material with J > 150 meV can be made metallic. The localization-exchange trade-off is fundamental: strong exchange requires localized spins, but superconductivity requires itinerant electrons.

## Task 4: Track A verdict

**CLOSES NEGATIVELY.** Best candidates are cuprates themselves at omega_sf ~ 350 K. No material family achieves omega_sf > 500 K in a metallic state.
