# Phase 67: High-J Materials Survey and omega_sf Computation

## Objective

Systematically survey material families for exchange coupling J > 150 meV (equivalently omega_sf > 500 K for S=1/2), compute omega_sf from first principles or literature values, and determine whether any metallic (or dopable) material exceeds the cuprate omega_sf ~ 350 K baseline. This is the entry point for Track A of v13.0.

## Context

- v12.0 baseline: omega_log_eff = 483 K, Tc = 197 K at mu*=0
- Cuprate J ~ 130 meV, omega_sf ~ 350 K (30 meV)
- Target: omega_sf > 500 K (43 meV) requires J > 150 meV for S=1/2
- Formula: omega_sf = 2*sqrt(2)*J*S (mean-field zone-boundary magnon)

## Tasks

### Task 1: Literature survey of exchange couplings across material families
type="auto"
verify="analytical"

Compile J values from established literature for:
1. Cuprates (Hg1223, YBCO, LSCO, Bi2212, Tl2201, HgBa2CuO4)
2. Iridates (Sr2IrO4, Ba2IrO4) -- J_eff=1/2 with SOC
3. Ruthenates (Sr2RuO4, SrRuO3)
4. Iron pnictides/chalcogenides (LaFeAsO, BaFe2As2, FeSe)
5. Chromium trihalides (CrI3, CrCl3, CrBr3)
6. Nickel phosphorus trisulfide (NiPS3)
7. High-valent 3d oxides (SrMnO3, LaCrO3, LaVO3)
8. Heavy fermion (UPd2Al3, CeCoIn5)
9. Nickelates (La3Ni2O7, NdNiO2)
10. Additional: La2CuO4 variants, transition-metal nitrides, borides

For each: record J (meV), spin quantum number S, electronic state (metallic/insulating/Mott), superconducting? (Y/N with Tc if Y), source.

Done: Table with >= 25 entries spanning >= 8 material families.

### Task 2: Compute omega_sf for all surveyed materials
type="auto"
verify="numerical"

For each material in the Task 1 table:
- Compute omega_sf = 2*sqrt(2)*J*S in K (using k_B = 8.617e-2 meV/K)
- Identify which materials exceed omega_sf > 500 K
- Identify which materials exceed omega_sf > 350 K (cuprate baseline)
- Flag materials that are metallic or dopable (potential superconductors)

Done: Extended table with omega_sf column; ranked by omega_sf descending.

### Task 3: Feasibility assessment -- J > 150 meV in metals
type="auto"
verify="analytical"

For any material with J > 150 meV:
- Is it metallic? If insulating, can it be doped to metallic?
- What happens to J upon doping? (J typically decreases as carriers screen exchange)
- Is there a known superconducting phase?
- Physical argument: why is high J fundamentally difficult in metals?

For materials with omega_sf > 350 K but J < 150 meV (i.e., higher S):
- Can multi-orbital enhancement provide effective J_eff > 150 meV?
- SOC-enhanced exchange in 5d systems?

Done: Feasibility verdict for each candidate with omega_sf > 350 K.

### Task 4: Track A verdict and comparison with cuprate baseline
type="auto"
verify="analytical"

Produce:
1. Final ranked table: material | J (meV) | S | omega_sf (K) | metallic? | SC? | feasibility verdict
2. Explicit comparison: best candidate omega_sf vs cuprate omega_sf = 350 K
3. Track A go/no-go assessment: is there any material family worth pursuing for omega_sf > 500 K?
4. If Track A closes negatively: document why J > 150 meV is incompatible with metallic character, citing specific physical mechanisms

Done: Track A preliminary verdict with supporting evidence.

## Verification

- All J values carry units of meV with sources cited
- All omega_sf in K, computed via omega_sf = 2*sqrt(2)*J*S with k_B conversion
- Dimensional consistency: J (meV) * S (dimensionless) -> omega_sf (meV) -> K via k_B
- Cuprate baseline (J ~ 130 meV, omega_sf ~ 350 K) reproduced as a consistency check
- At least 25 materials surveyed across >= 8 families
