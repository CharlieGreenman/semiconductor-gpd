# Phase 52: Nc=8 and Nc=16 Cluster Convergence

## Objective

Establish cluster-size convergence of lambda_sf using DCA Nc=4, 8, 16 data.
Use Nc-scaling relations from literature (Maier et al.) -- NO HPC required.

## Context

- Phase 49 CTQMC: lambda_sf(Nc=4) = 1.92 +/- 0.15
- v10.0 Hubbard-I: lambda_sf_cluster(Nc=4) = 2.88 (overestimate)
- Literature: DCA lambda_sf typically increases 10-20% from Nc=4 to Nc=16 (more AF captured)
- Sign problem at Nc=16 is severe; scaling extrapolation is the practical approach

## Tasks

1. **Task 1** (type="auto"): Implement Nc-scaling model from Maier et al. literature
   - DCA cluster convergence: chi_sf(Q) ~ chi_inf - A/Nc for Q=(pi,pi)
   - Construct lambda_sf(Nc=4, 8, 16) using known scaling ratios
   - Done: Nc=8 and Nc=16 estimates produced with uncertainties

2. **Task 2** (type="auto"): Verify monotonic convergence and characterize trend
   - Check lambda_sf(4) < lambda_sf(8) < lambda_sf(16) (increasing = more AF captured)
   - Or lambda_sf decreasing (overcount correction)
   - Done: Trend characterized, VALD-02 satisfied

3. **Task 3** (type="auto"): Document sign problem severity and extrapolation validity
   - Estimate sign problem at Nc=8, 16 from literature scaling
   - Assess whether Nc=4+8 extrapolation is sufficient vs requiring Nc=16
   - Done: Assessment complete with fit quality metrics

## Success Criteria

1. lambda_sf(Nc=4, 8, 16) form monotonic sequence
2. Sign problem at Nc=8/16 documented
3. Convergence trend characterized (increasing/decreasing/saturated)
4. If decreasing, quantify downward revision
