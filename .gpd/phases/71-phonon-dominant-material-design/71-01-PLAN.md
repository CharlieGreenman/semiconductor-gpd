# Phase 71: Phonon-Dominant Material Design

## Objective

Design light-element oxide/hydride materials with phonon-dominant pairing (lambda_ph >> lambda_sf) and compute their electron-phonon coupling, establishing whether the phonon-dominant route can sustain omega_log_eff > 700 K.

## Context

- v12.0 baseline: omega_log_eff = 483 K, Tc = 197 K (Allen-Dynes, mu*=0)
- Target: omega_log_eff = 740 K for Tc = 300 K at lambda_total = 3.5
- Track C idea: flip to phonon-dominant regime where lambda_ph >> lambda_sf, so omega_log_eff stays near omega_ph (~852 K for H modes)
- Key tradeoff: phonon-dominant materials are s-wave, so mu* = 0.10 (not 0)

## Tasks

### Task 1: Literature survey of phonon-dominant high-Tc candidates
type="auto" verify="analytical"

Survey known high-lambda_ph materials at moderate pressures (< 50 GPa):
- H3S (200 GPa): lambda=2.2, omega_log=1300 K -- too high pressure
- LaH10 (150 GPa): lambda=2.9, omega_log=1500 K -- too high pressure
- CaH6 (150 GPa): lambda~2.7 -- too high pressure
- SH3 at lower pressures?
- Layered hydrogen intercalation compounds at 10-30 GPa
- Metal hydrides with cage/clathrate structures

Identify candidates with lambda_ph > 2.0 achievable at P < 50 GPa.
Tabulate: compound, structure, lambda_ph, omega_ph_log, pressure, E_hull, source.

### Task 2: Design 4 phonon-dominant candidate materials
type="auto" verify="numerical"

Design candidates targeting:
- lambda_ph > 2.5, omega_ph_log > 700 K
- Weak correlations (U/W < 0.5) so lambda_sf < 0.5
- Stability at < 50 GPa (ideally < 20 GPa)

Candidate families to explore:
1. Heavily hydrogenated La3Ni2O7 (H1.0) -- weaker correlations with more H
2. LiH-based layered compounds -- very light, high omega_ph
3. CaH2/SrH2 layered intercalation compounds -- known high lambda_ph at lower P
4. H-clathrate in oxide framework (e.g., BaH8-type cages in perovskite)

For each: estimate lambda_ph from Hopfield parameter eta = N(E_F) * <I^2>,
omega_ph_log from H-mode frequencies, and correlation strength U/W.

### Task 3: Compute omega_log_eff and Allen-Dynes Tc for all candidates
type="auto" verify="numerical"

For each candidate:
- omega_log_eff = exp[(lambda_ph*ln(omega_ph) + lambda_sf*ln(omega_sf))/lambda_total]
- Allen-Dynes Tc at mu*=0.10 (s-wave) and mu*=0.00 (hypothetical d-wave)
- Compare to v12.0 baseline (483 K, 197 K)
- Identify any candidate with Tc > 250 K

### Task 4: Stability and feasibility assessment
type="auto" verify="analytical"

For each candidate:
- E_hull estimate (meV/atom) -- thermodynamic stability
- Phonon stability check -- imaginary frequencies?
- Migdal validity: omega_log/E_F ratio
- Synthesis feasibility at target pressure

Produce final candidate table meeting Phase 71 success criteria.

## Verification

- All lambda values dimensionless
- All omega values in K (with meV conversion stated)
- All Tc values in K
- Dimensional consistency in Allen-Dynes formula
- Benchmark: Allen-Dynes reproduces known H3S Tc within 15%
- At least 3 candidates with lambda_ph > 2.5 and omega_ph_log > 700 K
