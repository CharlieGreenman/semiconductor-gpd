# Phase 14 Route Longlist

## Ranked Longlist

| Rank | Route class | Confidence | Best carried ambient or retained `Tc` | Room-temperature gap | Why it stays live | Main failure mode |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | `Hg1223`-class pressure-quenched multilayer cuprates | high | `151 K` | `149 K` | shortest gap in the carried experimental set; retained ambient operation already demonstrated | protocol opacity, reproducibility, and toxicity |
| 2 | strain plus oxygen-controlled bilayer nickelate films | medium-high | `45 K` onset | `255 K` | richest current discovery platform with multiple coupled knobs at ambient pressure | thin-film sensitivity, lower `Tc`, and processing complexity |
| 3 | pressure-quench methodology platforms (`BST`, `FeSe`) | high as method, medium as target | `37 K` in carried examples | `263 K` or larger | best route for understanding retention variables and structural memory | too far from room temperature to be the primary target |
| 4 | mature ambient baselines (`MgB2`-class thinking) | very high | `39 K` | `261 K` | practical, reproducible, and useful as engineering sanity checks | far from the room-temperature goal |
| 5 | hydride and framework watchlist (`CsInH3`, `KB3C3`, `RbPH3`) | low | mixed | mixed | scientifically useful as controls or speculative watchlist entries | blocked by v2.0 no-go, weak ambient retention evidence, or theory-only status |

## Why The Longlist Keeps Negatives

The longlist is not a hype table. It preserves:

- the strongest benchmark route
- the richest discovery platform
- the best methodology platforms
- a practical baseline
- the routes that should remain downgraded

That full space is what makes the final recommendation defensible.
