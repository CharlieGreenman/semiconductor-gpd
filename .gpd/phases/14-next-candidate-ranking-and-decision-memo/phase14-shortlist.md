# Phase 14 Shortlist

## Ranked Shortlist

| Route class | Benchmark confidence | Discovery richness | Room-temperature proximity | Practical burden | Verdict |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `Hg1223`-class pressure-quenched multilayer cuprates | highest | medium-high | highest in carried set | high | `primary route` |
| strain plus oxygen-controlled bilayer nickelate films | medium-high | highest | medium | medium-high | `backup route` |
| PQP methodology platforms (`BST`, `FeSe`) | high as method | medium | low | medium | `method platform only` |

## Why The Primary Route Is Hg1223-Class

- it has the best current benchmark proximity to room temperature
- it already demonstrates retained ambient operation after pressure history
- the descriptor model does not reject it; it only penalizes protocol opacity

## Why The Backup Route Is Bilayer Nickelates

- it has the richest knob surface in the carried ambient set
- it is easier to probe, iterate, and tune at ambient pressure
- even though the current `Tc` is much lower, it may unlock more new science per iteration

## Route Priority Statement

If the target is `highest current confidence toward the room-temperature goal`, choose `Hg1223`-class pressure-quenched cuprates.

If the target is `fastest route to new controllable science`, bilayer nickelates are the strongest backup and platform route.
