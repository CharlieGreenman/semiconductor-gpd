# Phase 75 Plan: Orbital-Resolved Coupling and H-Intercalated Tc Prediction

## Objective

For the top nickelate candidates from Phase 74 (NdNiO2, La3Ni2O7, La4Ni3O10), compute:
1. Orbital-resolved electron-phonon coupling lambda_ph(dz2) and electron-SF coupling lambda_sf(dx2-y2)
2. Design H-intercalated variants with H in the rare-earth spacer layer
3. Compute omega_log_eff for the H-intercalated structures
4. Solve anisotropic Eliashberg Tc with two-orbital kernel
5. Assess whether orbital selectivity genuinely decouples d-wave and phonon channels enough for Tc >= 300 K

## Context

- Phase 74 identified nickelates as best OS candidates: dx2-y2 (correlated) + dz2 (itinerant)
- v13.0 target: lambda_ph >= 3.0 + d-wave (mu*=0) + omega_log_eff >= 740 K for Tc = 300 K
- v12.0 baseline: omega_log_eff = 483 K, Tc = 197 K (Allen-Dynes)
- Key test: lambda_ph(dz2 itinerant) > 2.0 AND dx2-y2 supports d-wave eigenvalue > 0

## Tasks

### Task 1: Orbital-Resolved Coupling Model (type="auto")
Build a two-orbital Eliashberg model with:
- Orbital 1 (dx2-y2): correlated, Z ~ 0.25, coupled to spin fluctuations (lambda_sf)
- Orbital 2 (dz2): itinerant, Z ~ 0.55, coupled to phonons (lambda_ph)
- Interorbital hybridization V_12 as a parameter controlling OS decoupling
Compute orbital-resolved lambda_ph and lambda_sf from model parameters calibrated to DFT/DMFT literature.
**done:** Two-orbital model with orbital-resolved couplings for NdNiO2, La3Ni2O7, La4Ni3O10.

### Task 2: H-Intercalated Structure Design (type="auto")
Design H-intercalated variants:
- NdNiO2-Hx: H in Nd spacer layer (interstitial sites between Nd atoms)
- La3Ni2O7-Hx: H in La-O spacer layer
- Estimate H phonon frequencies from DFT scaling (omega_H ~ 100-150 meV for H in oxide cages)
- Estimate lambda_ph(dz2, H-modes) from dz2 DOS at E_F and deformation potential
- Check E_hull feasibility (order-of-magnitude estimate)
**done:** H-intercalated candidates with estimated lambda_ph(dz2) and omega_H.

### Task 3: omega_log_eff Computation (type="auto")
Compute the effective logarithmic average frequency for the H-intercalated structures:
- Use the orbital-resolved formula where lambda_sf is concentrated on dx2-y2 (low omega_sf ~ 350 K)
  and lambda_ph is concentrated on dz2 (high omega_H ~ 1000-2000 K)
- The key hypothesis: because SF and phonons couple to DIFFERENT orbitals,
  omega_log_eff is dominated by the phonon channel (not dragged down by SF)
- Compare with the single-orbital formula where both channels share the same omega_log_eff
**done:** omega_log_eff for each H-intercalated candidate, both orbital-resolved and single-orbital for comparison.

### Task 4: Anisotropic Eliashberg Tc (type="auto")
Solve the two-orbital anisotropic Eliashberg equation:
- d-wave gap on dx2-y2 (from SF coupling): delta_1(k) = delta_0 * (cos kx - cos ky)
- s-wave or isotropic gap on dz2 (from phonon coupling): delta_2(k) = delta_0'
- Interorbital pair transfer: how much of the d-wave pairing leaks to dz2 via hybridization
- mu*: 0 for the d-wave channel (dx2-y2), 0.10-0.13 for the s-wave channel (dz2)
- THE CRITICAL QUESTION: does the total Tc benefit from having phonons on dz2, or does mu* on the phonon channel limit the enhancement?
**done:** Anisotropic Eliashberg Tc for each candidate with uncertainty estimate.

### Task 5: Track A Assessment and 300 K Verdict (type="auto")
Consolidate all results into a Track A verdict:
- Does orbital selectivity genuinely decouple d-wave and phonon channels?
- What is the maximum Tc achievable with the orbital-selective mechanism?
- How does this compare with the 300 K target?
- Quantify the shortfall if Tc < 300 K
- Identify the fundamental limitation
**done:** Track A verdict with quantified Tc and gap to 300 K.

## Verification

- Orbital-resolved couplings must be consistent with single-orbital limits
- omega_log_eff must reduce to known formulas in limit of zero hybridization
- Eliashberg Tc must be consistent with Allen-Dynes in isotropic limit
- All energies in eV/meV, temperatures in K, frequencies in meV or K
