# Phase 22 Negative-Control Note

This note screens out the route classes that should stay out of top post-`v5.0` contention unless new evidence changes the picture materially.

## Rejected Or Downgraded Classes

| Route class | Why it stays out of top contention |
| --- | --- |
| pressure-only routes with no retained or ambient benchmark | they do not yet shrink the practical operating-state gap |
| onset-only routes without enough headroom or supporting evidence | they can be scientifically interesting without being the best next route |
| theory-only near-ambient conventional optimism | current ceiling and evidence do not beat the unconventional frontier |
| hydride routes already closed negatively in `v1.0` and `v2.0` | the repo already tested and downgraded them under stricter practical criteria |

## Survivors

- `Hg`-family cuprates survive because they still lead on absolute `Tc` headroom and already include a retained `151 K` benchmark.
- Nickelates survive because they now combine real ambient operation with multiple active uplift levers and a rapidly moving pressure frontier.

## Guardrail

Screening out weak classes does not mean the survivors are close to room temperature. It only means they still deserve the next route-program pass.
