# Phase 22 Control-Knob Matrix

This matrix records the highest-upside uplift levers for the route families that still matter after `v5.0`.

## Route-Control Matrix

| Route family | Highest-upside control knobs | Why they matter | Main risk | Strategic reading |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `Hg`-family cuprates | pressure-quench transfer, multilayer optimization, inner/outer plane differentiation, oxygen tuning, reservoir-layer chemistry that preserves flat `CuO2` planes | the family already sits closest to room temperature in absolute `Tc`, so even modest retained uplift matters | transfer from pressure headroom to retained ambient operation may remain narrow | best absolute headroom, weaker control richness than nickelates |
| bilayer and related nickelates | compressive strain, hydrostatic pressure, oxygen stoichiometry, rare-earth substitution as chemical pressure, bilayer/trilayer structural choice | the route has multiple active knobs that are still visibly moving the frontier upward | best current `Tc` still remains far below `Hg1223` retained scale | best route for control-rich expansion and discovery leverage |
| conventional near-ambient controls | chemical precompression, framework choice, electron count tuning | useful as a bounded control problem | present ceiling and evidence do not compete with the unconventional frontier | control-only, not top priority |

## Route Asymmetry

- `Hg`-family cuprates lead on **absolute headroom**.
- Nickelates lead on **control richness and current frontier-improvement rate**.
- No current route leads at everything, so the next milestone must choose deliberately rather than by headline alone.

## Guardrail

This matrix identifies knobs. It does not claim that any knob already closes the `149 K` room-temperature gap.
