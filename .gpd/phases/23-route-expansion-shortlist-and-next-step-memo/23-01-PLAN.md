  <name>Task 1: Build weighted multi-criteria ranking table</name>
  <files>.gpd/phases/23-route-expansion-shortlist-and-next-step-memo/phase23-weighted-ranking.md, .gpd/phases/23-route-expansion-shortlist-and-next-step-memo/phase23-weighted-ranking.json</files>
  <action>
    Read Phase 22 frontier-headroom-map, control-knob-matrix, and negative-control-note. DO NOT re-derive any Tc values or re-screen routes. Use Phase 22 outputs directly.

    Build a ranking table scoring Hg-family cuprates and nickelates on 5 axes. For each axis, assign a score on a 1-5 scale with explicit justification:

    **A1: Absolute Tc headroom (weight 0.30)**
    - Hg-family: 151 K retained ambient (zero-resist), 153 K zero-resist under pressure, 166 K onset under pressure. Gap = 300 - 151 = 149 K.
    - Nickelates: ~63 K ambient film onset, ~40 K ambient bulk zero-resist, 96 K onset at >20 GPa. Gap = 300 - 63 = 237 K (best ambient onset) or 300 - 40 = 260 K (best ambient bulk).
    - Scoring: Hg-family gets highest score (closest to 300 K). Nickelates score proportionally lower.

    **A2: Evidence depth (weight 0.25)**
    - Hg-family: decades of cuprate literature, but PQP is single-group with 3-day stability at 77 K and deterioration at 200 K.
    - Nickelates: 5 years of rapid data, multiple independent groups (Sun et al., Wang et al., film groups), but sub-families are fragmented.
    - Score on: independent reproductions, measurement types (resistivity, Meissner, specific heat), sample quality.

    **A3: Controllable lever count (weight 0.20)**
    - Hg-family: PQP transfer, multilayer optimization, oxygen tuning, reservoir-layer chemistry. Count from Phase 22 control-knob matrix.
    - Nickelates: compressive strain, hydrostatic pressure, oxygen stoichiometry, rare-earth substitution, structural choice (bilayer/trilayer/infinite-layer). Count from Phase 22 control-knob matrix.
    - Score on: number of named, experimentally demonstrated uplift levers.

    **A4: Operating-pressure feasibility (weight 0.15)**
    - Hg-family: best benchmark (151 K) is at retained ambient. Score: high.
    - Nickelates: best benchmarks require >20 GPa or are ambient but much lower Tc. Score based on best ambient Tc vs. best pressurized Tc gap.

    **A5: Ambient retention pathway clarity (weight 0.10)**
    - Hg-family: PQP demonstrated but metastable (3-day at 77 K, degrades at 200 K). Score: moderate.
    - Nickelates: ambient operation exists natively (SmNiO2 at 40 K, films at 63 K) but pressure-enhanced Tc (96 K) has no retention pathway yet. Score: moderate-to-low.

    Compute weighted total = sum(w_i * score_i) for each route. State which route is primary (higher score) and which is secondary (lower score).

    CRITICAL constraints:
    - Every Tc value must state whether it is zero-resistance or onset, and whether it is at ambient, retained-ambient, or under pressure (VALD-01).
    - The 149 K gap must appear in the headroom axis discussion.
    - Do NOT reopen the Phase 22 screening. Only Hg-family and nickelates are scored.
    - Nickelate sub-families must be named where relevant (bilayer La3Ni2O7-class, infinite-layer SmNiO2-class, trilayer La4Ni3O10-class).

    Write both .md (human-readable table with justifications) and .json (machine-readable with all scores, weights, and totals).
  </action>
  <verify>

    2. Weights sum to 1.00: 0.30 + 0.25 + 0.20 + 0.15 + 0.10 = 1.00.
    3. Weighted totals are arithmetically correct (recompute from scores and weights).
    4. Every Tc claim separates synthesis pressure from operating pressure (VALD-01).

  <action>
    Using the ranking table from Task 1, test the robustness of the primary/secondary assignment.

    For each of the 5 axes, vary its weight by +20% and -20% (relative to default), renormalize all weights to sum to 1.00, and recompute weighted totals. That gives 10 perturbation scenarios.

    Record for each scenario: perturbed weights (renormalized), weighted totals for both routes, and whether the primary/secondary assignment flips.

    Summarize:
    - If the ranking NEVER flips across all 10 scenarios: the ranking is robust. State this explicitly.
    - If the ranking flips in 1-3 scenarios: the ranking is moderately robust. State which weight changes cause the flip.
    - If the ranking flips in 4+ scenarios: the ranking is fragile. Recommend using the qualitative Phase 22 asymmetry argument as the fallback (per RESEARCH.md Approach 2).

    If fragile (ranking flips with <10% relative weight change on any single axis): note that the quantitative ranking does not cleanly distinguish the routes, and recommend co-primary status with separate next-step campaigns, per the decision criterion in RESEARCH.md.

    Append the sensitivity results to the existing .md and .json files.
  </action>
