    SECTION 1 -- SUB-FAMILY 1: BILAYER La3Ni2O7-CLASS (Ruddlesden-Popper n=2) [LEAD]

    Compile a complete data summary:
    - Lead materials: La3Ni2O7, (La,Pr)3Ni2O7, La2PrNi2O7, La2.82Sr0.18Ni2O7
    - Best ambient Tc: ~63 K ONSET in (La,Pr)3Ni2O7 GAE film on SLAO (arXiv:2512.04708). Zero-resist at ambient: only ~2-3 K in PLD films on SLAO (Ko et al. Nature 2024). GAE zero-resist: NOT REPORTED (critical gap).
    - Best pressurized Tc: 96 K onset / 73 K zero-resist in single crystals at >20 GPa (Wang et al. Nature 2025)
    - Lever stacking: strain + pressure yields ~48.5 K onset at 9 GPa on SLAO (Nature Commun. 2026)
    - Room-temperature gap: 300 - 63 = 237 K (ambient onset) or 300 - 3 = 297 K (ambient zero-resist in films)
    - Strengths: highest frontier Tc, richest control surface (5 levers), fastest-moving frontier
    - Weaknesses: enormous onset-zero gap (37-60 K), GAE method limited availability, zero-resist data sparse

    SECTION 2 -- SUB-FAMILY 2: INFINITE-LAYER SmNiO2-CLASS [BACKUP]

    - Lead material: SmNiO2 (bulk)
    - Best ambient Tc: ~40 K zero-resist in bulk SmNiO2 (Sun et al., Nature 2025, s41586-025-08893-4). Lower-quality samples: ~31 K zero-resist.
    - Operating conditions: ambient pressure, bulk, thermodynamically stable, air-stable
    - Room-temperature gap: 300 - 40 = 260 K
    - Strengths: truly ambient and stable, zero-resist confirmed (not onset-only), no pressure or strain required
    - Weaknesses: lower absolute Tc than bilayer, no thin-film strain engineering demonstrated, limited uplift levers explored
    - Note: NdNiO2 and related infinite-layer materials exist but with lower Tc (~15 K); SmNiO2 is the clear leader

    SECTION 3 -- SUB-FAMILY 3: TRILAYER La4Ni3O10-CLASS (Ruddlesden-Popper n=3) [LOW PRIORITY]

    - Lead material: La4Ni3O10
    - Best pressurized Tc: ~30 K onset at 69 GPa (literature)
    - Ambient Tc: not superconducting at ambient pressure
    - Room-temperature gap: 300 - 30 = 270 K (under extreme pressure; not practical)
    - Strengths: confirms RP series as superconducting family
    - Weaknesses: highest required pressure (69 GPa), lowest Tc, no ambient pathway demonstrated
    - Note: some reports of SC signatures in thin films but not confirmed to the level of the bilayer family

    SECTION 4 -- RANKING RATIONALE

    Explain why bilayer > infinite-layer > trilayer using ONLY concrete data:
    - Bilayer leads on: highest frontier Tc (96 K onset pressurized, 63 K onset ambient film), most active levers demonstrated (strain + pressure + composition), fastest frontier improvement rate (from 0 to 63 K onset in ~18 months)
    - Infinite-layer is backup because: stable ambient zero-resist (40 K) but lower ceiling, fewer demonstrated levers, no thin-film strain engineering explored yet
    - Trilayer is low priority because: lowest Tc (30 K), highest required pressure (69 GPa), no ambient pathway
    - State explicitly: this ranking could change if (1) infinite-layer exceeds 60 K zero-resist ambient, or (2) trilayer films demonstrate ambient SC
