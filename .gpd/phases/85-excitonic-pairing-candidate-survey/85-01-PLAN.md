# Phase 85: Excitonic Pairing Candidate Survey

## Objective

Survey materials with low-energy excitons (< 100 meV) adjacent to or coexisting with
metallic bands. Identify candidates for Phase 86 excitonic pairing calculation.

## Context

- v14.0 Eliashberg ceiling: 240 +/- 30 K; 60-90 K gap to 300 K
- Little (1964) / Ginzburg (1964) excitonic pairing: polarizable side-chains mediate attraction
- Allender-Bray-Bardeen (1973): formal excitonic mechanism theory
- Key challenge: excitons live in insulators, but pairing needs a metal

## Tasks

### Task 1: Compile excitonic material survey table (type="auto")

Survey these families:
- Excitonic insulator candidates near metal-insulator transition: 1T-TiSe2, Ta2NiSe5, TmSe0.45Te0.55
- Semiconductor/metal heterostructures: InAs/GaSb, WTe2/NbSe2, WS2/NbSe2
- Mixed-valence compounds: SmB6, YbB12, SmS
- Organic conductors: kappa-(BEDT-TTF)2X
- Cuprate-adjacent: doped Mott insulators with charge-transfer excitons

For each: omega_ex (meV), exciton binding energy (meV), N(E_F) (states/eV/f.u.),
metallic or insulating, existing Tc (K), feasibility for excitonic pairing.

### Task 2: Select best candidates for Phase 86 (type="auto")

Filter: omega_ex < 100 meV AND (metallic OR near-metallic under pressure/doping).
Rank by coupling potential: lower omega_ex is better (larger lambda_ex for same coupling)
but system must be metallic.

Identify top 2-3 candidates with justification.

### Task 3: Assess excitonic pairing feasibility (type="auto")

For selected candidates, estimate the excitonic coupling constant:
lambda_ex ~ |g_ex|^2 * N(E_F) / omega_ex

Using order-of-magnitude estimates for g_ex from the exciton-electron coupling.
Distinguish between genuine excitonic pairing (boson-mediated attraction) and
excitonic instability (competitor to SC).

## Success Criteria

- Survey covers >= 4 material families
- >= 10 materials tabulated with omega_ex, N(E_F), metallic status
- >= 2 candidates selected for Phase 86
- Key distinction made: excitonic pairing vs excitonic instability
