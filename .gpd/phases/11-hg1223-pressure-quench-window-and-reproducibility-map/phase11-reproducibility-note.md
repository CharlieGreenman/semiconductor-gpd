# Phase 11 Reproducibility Note

## Verdict

`Hg1223` should be carried forward as:

- a `high-confidence benchmark` for retained ambient high-`Tc`
- a `medium-confidence protocol map` because the carried abstract is still sparse on the exact quench window
- a `low-confidence reproducibility window` until the sample-count, `P_Q`, `T_Q`, and lifetime details are surfaced more fully

## Why This Is The Right Trust Split

`Hg1223` is stronger than the analog set on ambient `Tc`, but weaker than `BST` and `FeSe` on openly surfaced protocol detail.

- `BST` exposes explicit `P_Q`, `T_Q`, phase labels, room-temperature DAC recovery, and thermal or temporal stability tests.
- `FeSe` exposes exact `P_Q` and `T_Q` examples, retained `Tc` up to `37 K`, and survival to `300 K` for at least `7 d` in some cases.
- `Hg1223` clearly establishes the existence of retained ambient high-`Tc` superconductivity, but the carried abstract does not yet expose the same parameter richness.

## Load-Bearing Variables To Carry Forward

1. `P_Q`: the source phase region matters
2. `T_Q`: retention quality changes with the quench temperature
3. structural-memory or phase-boundary context: coexistence, sluggish transformation, or electronically driven transitions appear favorable
4. ambient stability test: room-temperature recovery, aging, or cycling must be reported

## What The Repo Needs Next For Hg1223

- exact `P_Q` sweep or best-known quench band
- exact `T_Q` sweep or best-known recovery temperature
- zero-resistance and Meissner reproducibility statistics across samples
- ambient lifetime, thermal-cycling, and storage sensitivity
- explicit structural-memory marker that explains why the retained state survives decompression

## Phase 11 Outcome

Phase `11` upgrades the `Hg1223` story from "best benchmark" to "best benchmark with a partially opaque protocol." That is still enough to justify the route, but not enough to treat it as a mature engineering platform.
