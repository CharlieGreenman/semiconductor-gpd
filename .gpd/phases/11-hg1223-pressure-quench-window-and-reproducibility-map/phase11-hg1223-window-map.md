# Phase 11 Hg1223 Window Map

## Benchmark Ledger

| Field | Status | Current reading |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Material | known | `HgBa2Ca2Cu3O8+delta` |
| Evidence class | known | experimental |
| Ambient superconductivity after preparation | known | yes |
| Ambient `Tc` | known | `151 K` |
| `P_op` after preparation | known | `0 GPa` |
| Pressure-history method | known | pressure-quench protocol (`PQP`) |
| Structural support | known | synchrotron XRD plus phonon and electronic structure calculations support the retained state |
| Room-temperature gap | known | `149 K` |
| Exact `P_Q` sweep | unresolved in carried abstract | requires full-text parameter extraction |
| Exact `T_Q` sweep | unresolved in carried abstract | requires full-text parameter extraction |
| Sample-count statistics | unresolved in carried abstract | public summaries indicate repeated runs, but exact statistics are not surfaced here |
| Ambient lifetime or cycling window | unresolved in carried abstract | not surfaced in the carried abstract |

## What The Repo Can Already Say

- `Hg1223` is the strongest experimentally anchored retained-ambient benchmark in the repo's carried source set.
- The result is not a pressure-only claim: the superconducting state operates at `0 GPa` after preparation.
- The route is more than a transport-only anecdote because the paper reports supporting synchrotron XRD and electronic or phonon calculations.

## What Phase 11 Still Treats As Opaque

- the exact `P_Q` and `T_Q` values or sweep that maximize the retained state
- whether there is a narrow protocol window or a broader reproducibility basin
- the detailed sample-to-sample dispersion of the retained `Tc`
- the ambient stability window under repeated thermal cycling or storage

## Practical Reading

`Hg1223` is already benchmark-solid on the question "is retained ambient high-`Tc` superconductivity possible at all?" It is not yet protocol-solid on the question "what exact quench window should another group follow, and how narrow is that window?"
