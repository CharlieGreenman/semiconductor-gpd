## Summary

Phase `10` established `Hg1223` as the repo's highest-confidence benchmark, but that result is still too point-like. The next useful move is to ask what part of the `Hg1223` route is actually parameterized and what part remains opaque. The strongest comparison set is not another hydride benchmark; it is the pressure-quench protocol literature itself, especially `Bi0.5Sb1.5Te3` and `FeSe`, where `P_Q`, `T_Q`, phase coexistence, room-temperature recovery, and stability windows are surfaced much more explicitly.

That means Phase `11` should do two things at once:

- lock `Hg1223` into an exact known versus unknown variable ledger
- use `BST` and `FeSe` to show what a mature pressure-quench map looks like

## Phase 11 Working Question

What part of the `Hg1223` retained-superconductivity route is already decision-grade, what part is still protocol-opaque, and which pressure-quench variables look load-bearing across the best analog systems?
