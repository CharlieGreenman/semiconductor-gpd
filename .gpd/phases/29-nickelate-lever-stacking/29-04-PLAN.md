    Model the effect of rare-earth substitution on La3Ni2O7 structure and electron-phonon coupling at the optimal strain (expected: -2.01%, SLAO).

    1. Ionic radius-based chemical pressure model:
       - Shannon ionic radii (9-coordinate, relevant for La-site in RP perovskite):
         * La3+ = 1.160 A
         * Pr3+ = 1.126 A (2.9% smaller -> effective chemical pressure ~ 2-4 GPa equivalent)
         * Nd3+ = 1.109 A (4.4% smaller -> effective chemical pressure ~ 4-6 GPa equivalent)
         * Sm3+ = 1.079 A (7.0% smaller -> effective chemical pressure ~ 6-10 GPa equivalent)
       - RE substitution at the La site reduces the effective A-site radius, which:
         * Reduces c-axis (chemical compression along c)
         * May modify inner apical O position (closer to Ni -> stronger sigma bonding)
         * May affect octahedral tilts (tolerance factor change)
    2. Structural modeling approach (NO HPC):
       a. Use Vegard's law for lattice parameter estimation:
          - a(RE) ~ a(La327) * (1 - alpha * (r_La - r_RE)/r_La) where alpha ~ 0.5-1.0
          - c(RE) estimated similarly; c is more sensitive to A-site radius
       b. QE structure input files for ordered La2PrNi2O7 and La2NdNi2O7 (33% substitution):
          - Use 2x1x1 supercell with one La replaced by RE (or VCA in QE if available)
          - Fix a to SLAO substrate (a = 3.756 A) for the strained case
          - Relax c and internal coordinates
       c. Literature-expected effects:
          - Pr substitution: published (La,Pr)3Ni2O7 films show Tc_onset = 63 K on SLAO (vs 40 K for La3Ni2O7)
          - Nd substitution: less studied; expect intermediate between Pr and Sm effects
          - Sm substitution: Sm3Ni2O7 is a different compound with its own Tc ~ 40 K (bulk, ambient)
    3. Electronic structure estimation at each RE:
       a. N(E_F) change: primarily through lattice parameter modification (chemical pressure)
       b. dz2 weight change: through c/a modification
       c. Check for RE-4f states near E_F:
          - Pr: 4f^2 configuration, 4f states typically 2-3 eV below E_F (safe)
          - Nd: 4f^3, 4f states 2-4 eV below E_F (safe)
          - Sm: 4f^5, 4f states may approach E_F (flag as risk)
    4. lambda and Tc estimation for RE-substituted compounds:
       a. Use the strain-Tc relationship from Plan 29-03 as the base
       b. Estimate additional lambda change from chemical pressure using:
          lambda(RE) ~ lambda(strain_optimal) * (1 + d(ln lambda)/d(ln V) * delta_V/V)
          where delta_V/V is the volume change from RE substitution
          Published Gruneisen parameter for nickelate lambda: d(ln lambda)/d(ln V) ~ 1-3
       c. Compute Tc(RE, strain) for each RE at optimal strain point
    5. Generate Tc vs RE ionic radius figure at optimal strain.
    6. Write re_substitution_results.json with all data.

    KEY EXPERIMENTAL ANCHOR: (La,Pr)3Ni2O7 on SLAO shows Tc_onset = 63 K, which is HIGHER than La3Ni2O7 on SLAO (onset 40 K). This ~23 K enhancement from Pr substitution is the primary validation target for the chemical pressure model. However, zero-resistance Tc for (La,Pr)3Ni2O7 is NOT reported -- this is a critical unknown.
