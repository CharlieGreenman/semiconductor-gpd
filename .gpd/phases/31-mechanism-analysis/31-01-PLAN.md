# Plan 31-01: Mechanism Analysis and Cross-Track Synthesis

## Objective

Quantitative mechanism decomposition across all three tracks (Hg multilayer, nickelate lever-stacking, hybrid superlattice) answering: WHY does Tc go up or down in each track? What fraction of pairing is phonon-mediated vs spin-fluctuation? What does this tell us about where to look next?

## Context

- Phase 28 verdict: DECREASE. n=3 optimal. lambda_IP=0.29 < lambda_OP=0.45. AF kills n>=4.
- Phase 29 verdict: PHONON-PARTIAL. Best phonon-only Tc=26.3 K. 80K gate NOT met.
- Phase 30 verdict: MARGINAL. Best phonon-only Tc=21 K. Volume-dilution of lambda.
- Phase 27 baseline: Hg1223 phonon-only Tc=31 K (80% below 151 K experiment).

## Tasks

### Task 1: Hg multilayer mechanism decomposition (type="auto")

Quantify WHY n=3 is optimal:
- N(E_F) vs layer count
- lambda_OP vs lambda_IP decomposition
- AF inner-plane mechanism (NMR evidence: Mukuda et al.)
- Phonon mode attribution (apical-O vs in-plane Cu-O)
- Scaling: what fraction of lambda comes from each phonon branch?

### Task 2: Nickelate strain mechanism decomposition (type="auto")

Quantify WHY strain enhances Tc:
- dz2 orbital weight at E_F vs strain
- Phonon frequency shift vs strain (apical breathing mode)
- N(E_F) vs strain
- Chemical pressure (RE substitution) effect on c/a and dz2 overlap
- Estimated spin-fluctuation contribution range

### Task 3: Cross-track Tc summary and 149 K gap update (type="auto")

- Master table: all candidates on common basis (structure, pressure, mu*, lambda, omega_log, Tc_phonon, Tc_estimated_full, method)
- Phonon fraction analysis for each family
- 149 K gap explicit update
- What computation or experiment would be needed to capture full Tc?
- Requirements check: HG-04, NI-04, VALD-03

## Success Criteria

1. Hg multilayer trend explained by IP/OP lambda decomposition
2. Nickelate strain trend explained by dz2 orbital shift
3. All Tc values tabulated on common basis
4. 149 K gap explicitly updated
5. Phonon vs spin-fluctuation fraction estimated for each family
