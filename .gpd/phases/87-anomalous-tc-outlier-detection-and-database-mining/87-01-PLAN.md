# Phase 87: Anomalous-Tc Outlier Detection and Database Mining

## Objective

Identify superconductors where Tc significantly exceeds Eliashberg predictions
(delta_Tc > 30 K). Exclude known unconventional mechanisms (cuprate SF, heavy
fermion Kondo). Find genuinely UNEXPLAINED anomalies.

## Context

- v14.0 Eliashberg ceiling: 240 +/- 30 K
- Known anomalies: FeSe/STO (8x bulk Tc), MATBG (anomalous for carrier density)
- SuperCon database: ~30,000 entries; not all have Eliashberg predictions

## Tasks

### Task 1: Compile high-Tc materials with Eliashberg comparisons (type="auto")

Catalog all Tc > 30 K superconductors with available Eliashberg predictions.
For each: material | Tc_expt | Tc_Eliashberg | delta_Tc | mechanism (known/unknown).

### Task 2: Flag unexplained anomalies (type="auto")

Filter out materials where the mechanism IS known:
- Cuprates: spin-fluctuation mediated (known)
- Heavy fermions: Kondo/valence-fluctuation mediated (known)
- Organics: spin-fluctuation/Mott proximity (known)
- Hydrides under pressure: strong e-ph coupling (known, Eliashberg works)

Remaining: genuinely anomalous materials.

### Task 3: Characterize anomaly patterns (type="auto")

For each genuinely anomalous material: what makes it anomalous?
Interface effect? Substrate coupling? Proximity? Unusual band structure?
Identify common threads.

## Success Criteria

- >= 20 materials catalogued with Tc > 30 K and Eliashberg comparison
- Cuprates, HF, organics excluded as "mechanism known"
- Genuinely anomalous materials identified (may be zero)
- Pattern analysis if anomalies found
