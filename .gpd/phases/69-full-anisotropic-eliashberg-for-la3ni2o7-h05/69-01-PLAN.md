# Phase 69: Full Anisotropic Eliashberg for La3Ni2O7-H0.5

## Objective

Solve the full linearized anisotropic Eliashberg equation for La3Ni2O7-H0.5 with SEPARATE phonon and spin-fluctuation kernels. Determine whether momentum-resolved coupling gives higher Tc than the Allen-Dynes isotropic log-average (197 K from v12.0).

## Context

- v12.0 Allen-Dynes: omega_log_eff = 483 K, Tc = 197 K at mu*=0 (baseline)
- v10.0 d-wave solver: eta_aniso ~ 1.002-1.005 for Hg1223 (modest anisotropy boost)
- v12.0 Phase 62: La3Ni2O7-H0.5 combined Eliashberg Tc = 342 K (central, Allen-Dynes) / 291 K (corrected)
- Key physics: phonon coupling peaks at small q (forward scattering); SF coupling peaks at Q=(pi,pi). These couple to DIFFERENT FS regions. d-wave gap is large at antinode (SF strong) and zero at node (phonons contribute more).

## Parameters from v12.0

- lambda_ph = 1.27 (moderate H-O-Ni coupling)
- omega_ph = 852 K (73.4 meV)
- lambda_sf = 2.231 (central)
- omega_sf = 350 K (30.2 meV)
- lambda_total = 3.501
- mu* = 0 (d-wave Coulomb evasion)
- Tight-binding: 2-orbital bilayer nickelate model

## Tasks

1. **type="auto"** Build momentum-dependent coupling model
   - Construct tight-binding Fermi surface for bilayer La3Ni2O7
   - Define alpha2F_ph(k,k',omega): forward-scattering peaked, small-q dominated
   - Define alpha2F_sf(k,k',omega): AF nesting peaked at Q=(pi,pi), d-wave form factor
   - Verify: Fermi-surface averaged lambda_ph and lambda_sf reproduce v12.0 values
   - done: coupling model validated against isotropic averages

2. **type="auto"** Implement linearized anisotropic Eliashberg eigenvalue solver
   - Linearized gap equation: lambda_max(T) eigenvalue problem on the Fermi surface
   - Tc defined by lambda_max(Tc) = 1
   - Matsubara frequency sum with cutoff at 10*max(omega_ph, omega_sf)
   - d-wave symmetry imposed (B1g irrep)
   - done: solver runs and reproduces isotropic limit when anisotropy is turned off

3. **type="auto"** Solve for anisotropic Tc with separate kernels
   - Run solver with full momentum-dependent phonon + SF kernels
   - Determine Tc_aniso from eigenvalue crossing
   - Map Delta(k) on the Fermi surface at Tc
   - Convergence test: k-mesh 32, 64, 128 points on FS
   - done: Tc_aniso determined; Delta(k) map generated

4. **type="auto"** Verification and comparison
   - Compare Tc_aniso vs Tc_Allen-Dynes (197 K)
   - Identify physical mechanism: which FS regions drive the enhancement
   - Check limiting cases: (a) isotropic limit, (b) phonon-only, (c) SF-only
   - Quantify enhancement ratio R = Tc_aniso / Tc_AD
   - done: enhancement ratio quantified; mechanism identified

## Success Criteria

1. Anisotropic Tc determined with converged k-mesh
2. Direct comparison: Tc_aniso vs 197 K (Allen-Dynes baseline)
3. Delta(k) Fermi surface map showing d-wave anisotropy
4. Physical mechanism of enhancement identified
