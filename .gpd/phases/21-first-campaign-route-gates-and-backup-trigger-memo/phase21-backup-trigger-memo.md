# Phase 21 Backup Trigger Memo

The bilayer nickelate route remains the backup because it offers the best ambient-control knob surface in the carried set, not because it has overtaken `Hg1223` on retained `Tc`.

## Backup Activation Levels

| Level | Trigger | Route action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| stay backup | `Hg1223` reaches at least a valid `T1` headline-reproduction candidate or higher | keep nickelates as the preserved backup and continue monitoring the watchpoint |
| promote in parallel | `Hg1223` only reaches ambiguous or mixed `T1/T0` outcomes across the first campaign, with no `T2` basin support | start a more active parallel nickelate route while keeping `Hg1223` primary |
| co-primary trigger | `Hg1223` fails cleanly under countable evidence at the carried low-`TQ` nodes, or remains stuck below `T2` after clean follow-up | elevate nickelates to co-primary discovery status while keeping `Hg1223` as the retained-benchmark route |

## Why Nickelates Still Matter

- they remain the richest ambient-pressure control surface in the carried set
- the watchpoint literature continues to improve onset under ambient-pressure thin-film control
- they are still far below the `Hg1223` retained benchmark in `Tc`, so they are not an automatic replacement

## What Does Not Trigger Backup Promotion

- invalidated `Hg1223` runs
- one ambiguous miss
- missing `vQ` or thermal-path records
- vague concern that the route is "too hard"

The backup should activate more strongly only on clean evidence, not on frustration.
