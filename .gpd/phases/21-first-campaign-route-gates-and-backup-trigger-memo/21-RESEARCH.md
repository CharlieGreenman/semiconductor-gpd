# Phase 21 Research: First-Campaign Route Gates and Backup Trigger Memo

## Phase Decision

Phase `21` should not invent a new route. It should define how the repo updates confidence after the first instrumented `Hg1223` campaign without hiding either the `149 K` room-temperature gap or the nickelate backup.

## Why This Phase Matters

- The repo already has carried gates from Phase `17`, but they were written before the diagnostic vocabulary and evidence tiers from Phase `20`.
- Without a route-gate memo, the first instrumented campaign could still produce vague conclusions like "promising" or "concerning" without an explicit next move.
- The bilayer nickelate backup should be preserved honestly: it is still far below the `Hg1223` benchmark in `Tc`, but it remains the best ambient-control discovery platform in the carried set.

## Route-Update Consequences

1. A clean low-`TQ` failure with full logs is much stronger downgrade evidence than any invalid or ambiguous miss.
2. A clean `T1` headline reproduction candidate is enough to keep `Hg1223` primary, but not enough to call the route experimentally de-risked.
3. A `T2` basin-candidate signal keeps `Hg1223` primary and justifies more focused `Hg1223` work.
4. The nickelate backup should be promoted more aggressively only after clean `Hg1223` misses or persistent inability to move above `T1`.

## Consumer Guardrail

No route outcome in this phase authorizes room-temperature or consumer-hardware language. Even the strongest carried retained `Hg1223` benchmark remains `149 K` below room temperature.
