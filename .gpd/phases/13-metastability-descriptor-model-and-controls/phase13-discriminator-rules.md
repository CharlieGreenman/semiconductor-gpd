# Phase 13 Discriminator Rules

## Route-Selection Rules

1. A high-`Tc` route does not count as promising for this repo unless it already has `ambient-access` or a credible retention mechanism.
2. A retained or ambient route scores much better if its control variables are explicit and engineerable, not merely inferred.
3. Structural memory, hysteresis, or non-reconstructive tuning matter more than raw pressure reduction when judging whether a route can survive decompression or external control.
4. Discovery-rich routes are the ones with at least two usable knobs, not just one benchmark point.

## What These Rules Reject

- `CsInH3`-style results that are scientifically strong under pressure but unsupported at ambient pressure
- theory-only or weakly evidenced ambient claims such as the blocked `RbPH3` route
- route ranking by `Tc` alone

## Failure Modes

- The rule set could fail if the next breakthrough comes from a route with poor current controllability but unexpectedly easy synthesis.
- The rule set could fail if `Hg1223` turns out to have a broad, simple protocol window and the current opacity penalty is too harsh.
- The rule set could fail if thin-film nickelate sensitivity reflects sample pathology rather than a genuinely tunable superconducting route.

## Carry Forward

- Keep `pressure quench` and `strain plus oxygen control` as the only two knob families that deserve top-route consideration.
- Keep hydrides in the milestone only as negative controls unless new evidence overturns the `v2.0` no-go.
